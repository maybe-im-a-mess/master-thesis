\chapter{Experiments and Results}

This chapter presents the results of the experiments conducted to evaluate the performance of the proposed chatbot system. The results are organized according to the evaluation framework outlined in Chapter 4, covering information extraction quality, topic coverage, citation faithfulness, and mandatory question compliance. 
Each section includes a detailed analysis of the results, highlighting key findings and their implications for the research objectives. 
Results for Dataset A (60 conversations, GPT-5.4) and Dataset B (216 conversations, GPT-5.4-mini) are reported separately where relevant. 
The IE benchmarking is independent of the conversation datasets.

\subsection{Summary and Method Selection}

Balancing both accuracy (thresholded F1) and intrinsic quality (SUSWIR), the most suitable configuration for medical IE proved to be GPT-5 Mini with Naive prompting, which combines the top SUSWIR score (0.6563) with the second-highest F1 (0.5674), indicating strong source coverage and low redundancy while remaining highly accurate.
The absolute F1 leader, Ministral-14B with UIE (0.5715), ranks mid-pack on SUSWIR (0.6199), so it is preferable only if maximizing F1 alone is the primary objective.

\subsection{Qualitative Analysis}

Two excerpts illustrate the qualitative difference between the dialogue managers; full transcripts are provided in Appendix~\ref{app:mandatory_q_excerpts}. 
In the naive configuration (\textit{Geburtseinleitung}; persona Lotte, age 25, anxiety: medium, education: low; 9 turns; questions asked: 1/3; question recall: 0.33), the assistant stays focused on procedural details and does not elicit mandatory screening questions:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Patient:} ok, wie läuft die Geburtseinleitung genau ab? \\
\textbf{Assistant:} Gern. Die Geburtseinleitung kann je nach Befund unterschiedlich ablaufen: \\
\textbf{Patient:} [...] Wie merkt man denn, ob die Einleitung gut anschlägt? \\

\end{quote}

In the supervised configuration for the same procedure (8 turns; questions asked: 3/3; question recall: 1.00), the assistant explicitly inserts required questions, achieving full compliance:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Assistant:} [...] In welcher Schwangerschaftswoche befinden Sie sich aktuell? \\
\textbf{Patient:} Ich bin in der \textbf{39. Woche} und ehrlich gesagt etwas nervös.
\end{quote}

\section{Cross-Target Discussion}

This section briefly synthesizes the key cross-target findings; a detailed interpretation follows in Chapter 6.

\subsection{Information Extraction Quality vs. Topic Coverage}

The IE benchmarking (Section 5.1) identified GPT-5 Mini with Naive prompting as the strongest configuration. However, the Medical Assistant Agent typically communicated only 24--32\% of ground-truth topics in the naive setting (Section 5.2), indicating a substantial gap between extraction and communication.

\subsection{Impact of Supervised Dialogue Management}

Supervised dialogue management yielded consistent improvements across three of the four evaluation targets: topic coverage hit rates increased across all datasets and personas (Section 5.2), mandatory question compliance rose substantially (Section 5.4), and citation faithfulness remained stable or slightly improved (Section 5.3).

\subsection{Implications for Clinical Deployment}

In the naive configuration, the chatbot frequently missed mandatory screening questions and omitted the majority of required medical facts. These findings, alongside the gap between strict and relaxed citation precision, are discussed further in Chapter 6.
